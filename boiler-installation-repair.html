Common Questions

Boiler FAQs

Answers to the most common questions about boiler installation, repair and servicing.

How much does a new boiler installation cost?

A new boiler installation typically costs between £1,800 and £4,500 depending on the boiler type, brand, and complexity of the installation. Combi boilers are generally the most affordable option for smaller properties. We provide free, no-obligation quotes tailored to your home — just call or fill in our enquiry form.

How long does a boiler installation take?

A straightforward like-for-like boiler replacement typically takes 1 day. If we need to move the boiler location, upgrade pipework, or install a completely new central heating system, it can take 2–3 days. We'll always give you a clear timeline before any work begins.

Do you offer warranties on new boilers?

Yes. All new boiler installations come with the manufacturer's warranty, which ranges from 5 to 12 years depending on the brand and model. As accredited installers for Baxi, Vaillant, and Worcester Bosch, we can offer extended warranty periods on qualifying installations — something you won't always get from non-accredited installers.

What are the signs my boiler needs replacing?

Common signs include:

  • Frequent breakdowns or recurring faults
  • Rising energy bills despite normal usage
  • Strange banging, whistling, or gurgling noises
  • Uneven heating — some rooms hot, others cold
  • Yellow flame instead of blue (potential carbon monoxide risk)
  • Your boiler is over 10–15 years old
  • Replacement parts are becoming hard to find

If you're experiencing any of these, we can assess whether a repair or replacement makes more sense for you.

What's the difference between combi, system and conventional boilers?

Combi boiler — Heats water on demand directly from the mains. No separate hot water cylinder or cold water tank needed. Ideal for smaller homes and flats with 1–2 bathrooms.

System boiler — Works with a separate hot water cylinder (but no cold water tank in the loft). Better for larger homes with multiple bathrooms where you need hot water at several taps simultaneously.

Conventional boiler — Uses both a hot water cylinder and a cold water storage tank. Best suited for older properties with existing pipework, or homes with very high hot water demand.

Not sure which is right for you? We'll help you choose during a free home survey.
